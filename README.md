## 💡 Investment Thesis

### Thesis 1: Recycled-Resin Cost Moat

ADS sources ~46% of its inputs from recycled resin at 10-20% below virgin cost, structurally insulating margins from resin volatility. As Scope 3 disclosure mandates (California SB 253) take effect from 2027, specifiers will favour the lowest-carbon option. ADS already has it, and competitors cannot replicate the recycling network at scale.

### Thesis 2: Flooding Opens New Markets; NDS Fills The Gap

Flood risk is spreading into areas never built for stormwater infrastructure. The NDS acquisition (Feb 2026, ~$300M revenue) completes ADS's capture-to-treat product line and opens the residential sales channel in exactly the lower-US growth regions where flood demand is rising fastest and ADS has its lowest market share. Each flood event locks in permanent demand through FEMA remapping, building codes, and insurance requirements.

## ⚙️ Methodology

- **Valuation:** DCF-GGM with EV/EBITDA exit at 10.77x, WACC 8.45%, terminal growth 2.3%. Deliberately conservative with zero ESG premium priced in.
- **Research:** Synthesised 80+ Investor Day slides, 6 broker reports (UBS, Barclays, Deutsche Bank), SEC filings, and third-party climate data (NOAA, ASCE, EPA, First Street Foundation).
- **Fact-Checking:** 19 quantitative claims individually verified against primary sources. Adversarial thesis testing using Claude AI as a research co-pilot.
- **Risk Framework:** Three prioritised risks (NDS integration, federal funding freeze, ESG regulation dependency), each mapped to specific evidence-backed mitigations.
